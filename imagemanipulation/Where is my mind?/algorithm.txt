Each image contains only the following colors:

White  (0, 0, 0)
Red    (255, 0, 0) 
Green  (0, 255, 0) 
Blue   (0, 0, 255) 
Yellow (255, 255, 0) 
Cyan   (0, 255, 255)

The hash is a 6-digit number,

Take the sum of:
    Amount of red pixels * 2
    Amount of green pixels * 3
    Amount of blue pixels * 5
    Amount of yellow pixels * 7
    Amount of cyan pixels * 11
And set it the inital hash value.

Calculate which colors have prime amounts of pixels (Including white):
    If 0 colors have prime pixel counts, add the amount of red pixels to the hash
    If 1 color has prime pixel counts, add the amount of green pixels to the hash
    If 2 colors have prime pixel counts, add the amount of blue pixels to the hash
    If 3 or more colors have prime pixel counts, add the difference between the amount of yellow and cyan pixels to the hash.

Take the bitwise AND operation of the amount of red and green pixels, and add it to the hash
Take the bitwise OR operation of the maount of green and blue pixels, and add it to the hash
Take the bitwise XOR operation of the amount of yellow and cyan pixels, and subtract it from the hash
